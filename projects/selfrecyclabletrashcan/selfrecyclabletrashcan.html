Self Recyclable Trash Can

An intelligent prototype capable of automatically identifying and separating recyclable materials (metal, paper and plastic) using sensors and automation.

Arduino Color Sensors C++ Automation

Problem & Justification

Solid waste production is constantly growing. Despite awareness campaigns, the difficulty of proper sorting results in the waste of recyclable materials and increases environmental impact.

This project is justified by the need to encourage sustainable habits through accessible technology, making the recycling process practical and preventing common disposal errors.

Core Objectives

  • Automatically separate metal, paper, and plastic.
  • Propose a low-cost solution for domestic use.
  • Track and count materials for integration with public incentive systems.

Technical Methodology

1

Ideation & Pivot

Initially planned with AI via camera, the project pivoted to Color Sensors to ensure technical feasibility.

2

Mechanical Structure

Development of an inclined ramp where the material slides to a sorting point via gravity.

3

Logic & Sorting

The sensor recognizes colors. The Arduino activates servomotors that direct the waste to the correct bin.

Key Results & Social Impact

Integrated Tracking Software

Implementation of a real-time system that records every item processed allowing precise monitoring of recycling efficiency.

Municipal & Social Integration

A gamified proposal where reaching recycling goals triggers rewards like wheelchair donations or community events.

Scalability & Future
