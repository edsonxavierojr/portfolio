Self Recyclable Trash Can

An intelligent prototype designed for automatic identification and sorting of recyclables using low-latency sensors and Arduino logic.

Arduino Uno TCS230 Sensor C++ / Embedded Automation

Problem & Tech Solution

Solid waste production is constantly growing. Despite awareness campaigns, the difficulty of proper sorting results in the waste of recyclable materials and increases environmental impact.

The core challenge was to replace complex computer vision with an efficient, cost-effective hardware solution. This system utilizes light-frequency analysis to distinguish materials, making the process practical and preventing common disposal errors.

By focusing on color-coded waste streams, we achieved a response time of less than 500ms for material categorization.

Core Objectives

  • Automatically separate metal, paper, and plastic in real-time.
  • Propose a low-cost, modular hardware solution for domestic or community use.
  • Track and count materials (Serial Logging) for integration with public incentive systems.

Development Workflow

01

Ideation & Pivot

Initially planned with AI via camera, the project pivoted to TCS230 Color Sensors to ensure technical feasibility, lower costs, and near-instant response times.

02

Mechanical Structure

Development of a gravity-assisted inclined ramp. This design ensures consistent material positioning for accurate light-frequency readings.

03

Logic & Sorting

The system maps RGB values (e.g., Yellow for Metal). Arduino then triggers Dual-Servos to redirect the waste to the correct bin with zero manual input.

Key Results & Social Impact

Integrated Tracking Software

Implementation of a real-time system that records every item processed (metals, plastics), allowing precise monitoring of recycling efficiency via Arduino serial/data logs.

Municipal & Social Integration

A gamified proposal where neighborhoods reaching recycling goals trigger social rewards, such as **wheelchair donations** or community cultural events supported by local partners.

Scalability & Future Vision

  • Java-Based Dashboard

    Developing a robust desktop dashboard in Java to monitor city-wide waste distribution and sensor health.

  • Mobile App & Bluetooth

    Integration with a dedicated Android app for citizens to track their "Environmental Score" via Bluetooth/WiFi.

  • Energy Autonomy

    Implementation of solar panels to ensure 100% sustainable operation in public squares and schools.
